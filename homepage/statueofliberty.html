Statue of Liberty

About the Statue of Liberty

The Statue of Liberty is a colossal neoclassical sculpture located on Liberty Island in New York Harbor.

Dedicated on October 28, 1886, it was a gift from France to the United States, symbolizing freedom and democracy. Designed by French sculptor Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi and built by Gustave Eiffel, the statue stands at 151 feet tall, or 305 feet including its pedestal. It has become an enduring symbol of the United States and a welcoming sight for immigrants arriving from abroad.

The statue is made of copper and has developed a green patina over the years. Visitors can explore the grounds, visit the museum, and take a ferry ride for stunning views of the statue and the Manhattan skyline.

  • Gifted by: France
  • Location: Liberty Island, New York
  • Height: 151 feet (305 feet including pedestal)
  • Style: Neoclassical sculpture
  • Material: Copper and iron

Opening Hours

8:30 AM - 6:00 PM (hours vary by season)

Visiting Tips

  • Book tickets in advance to avoid long lines.
  • Consider taking a guided tour for a comprehensive experience.
  • Wear comfortable shoes as there's a lot of walking involved.
  • Bring a camera for beautiful photo opportunities.
