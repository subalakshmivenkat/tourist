Li River

About the Li River

The Li River is known for its stunning scenery and is one of the most picturesque rivers in China.

Flowing through Guangxi Zhuang Autonomous Region, the Li River is famous for its dramatic karst mountain landscape, which has inspired countless artists and poets throughout history. The river stretches approximately 83 kilometers (52 miles) from Guilin to Yangshuo, offering breathtaking views of limestone peaks, lush greenery, and traditional villages.

Cruises along the river are popular, allowing visitors to experience the stunning landscapes from the comfort of a boat. Hiking and cycling paths along the river provide opportunities for exploration and photography, making it a must-visit destination for nature lovers.

  • Length: Approximately 83 kilometers (52 miles)
  • Location: Guangxi Zhuang Autonomous Region, China
  • Best Time to Visit: April to October for pleasant weather
  • Famous For: Karst mountain scenery and traditional rural landscapes

Opening Hours

The Li River is accessible year-round, but boat cruises typically operate from 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM.

Visiting Tips

  • Book your river cruise in advance during peak season.
  • Bring a camera to capture the stunning landscapes.
  • Dress in layers, as temperatures can vary throughout the day.
  • Consider a bike ride or hike for a more immersive experience.
