Great Wall of China

About the Great Wall of China

The Great Wall of China is a series of fortifications made of various materials, built to protect Chinese states and empires from invasions.

Spanning over 13,170 miles (21,196 kilometers), it is one of the most iconic symbols of China and is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The wall was constructed over several dynasties, with the most famous sections built during the Ming Dynasty (1368â€“1644).

Visitors can hike along various parts of the wall, experiencing breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and landscapes. Some sections are well-preserved and easily accessible, while others remain rugged and less crowded, offering a more adventurous experience.

  • Length: Over 13,170 miles (21,196 kilometers)
  • Location: Northern China
  • Construction: Various dynasties, primarily Ming Dynasty
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site: Yes
  • Best Time to Visit: Spring and autumn for pleasant weather

Opening Hours

Typically 7:30 AM - 6:00 PM (hours may vary by section).

Visiting Tips

  • Wear comfortable shoes for hiking.
  • Bring water and snacks, especially for longer hikes.
  • Check the weather and dress accordingly.
  • Consider visiting less touristy sections for a quieter experience.
