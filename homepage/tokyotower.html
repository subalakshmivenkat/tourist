Tokyo Tower

About Tokyo Tower

Tokyo Tower is a communications and observation tower located in Minato, Tokyo, Japan.

Standing at 333 meters (1,092 feet), it is inspired by the Eiffel Tower and was completed in 1958. Tokyo Tower serves as a television broadcasting antenna and a major tourist attraction, offering stunning panoramic views of Tokyo from its observation decks.

The tower is painted white and international orange to comply with air safety regulations and features a distinctive lattice structure. Visitors can explore the Main Deck and the Top Deck, where they can enjoy breathtaking views of the city skyline, especially during sunset. The tower is illuminated at night, creating a beautiful sight against the Tokyo skyline.

  • Height: 333 meters (1,092 feet)
  • Location: Minato, Tokyo, Japan
  • Completed: 1958
  • Inspiration: Eiffel Tower
  • Use: Communications and observation

Opening Hours

9:00 AM - 11:00 PM (last admission at 10:30 PM)

Visiting Tips

  • Consider visiting during off-peak hours for a quieter experience.
  • Check the weather for clear views from the observation decks.
  • Purchase tickets online to save time at the entrance.
  • Don't miss the souvenir shops and cafes on the premises!
