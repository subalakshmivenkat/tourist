Qutub Minar

About the Qutub Minar

The Qutub Minar is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Delhi, India.

Standing at 73 meters tall, it is the tallest brick minaret in the world and is an important example of Indo-Islamic architecture. The construction of the minaret started in 1193 by Qutb al-Din Aibak, with further additions made by his successor Iltutmish. The intricate carvings and inscriptions on the tower showcase the architectural brilliance of that era.

The Qutub Minar is surrounded by a complex of historically significant structures, including the Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque, the first mosque built in India. Its red sandstone and marble construction gives it a unique charm, attracting visitors from all over the world.

  • Built by: Qutb al-Din Aibak
  • Location: Delhi, India
  • Height: 73 meters
  • Style: Indo-Islamic architecture
  • Material: Red sandstone and marble
