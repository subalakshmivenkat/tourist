Grand Canyon

About the Grand Canyon

The Grand Canyon is a vast canyon in Arizona, USA, carved by the Colorado River.

It is one of the most recognizable natural wonders in the world, stretching 277 miles long and up to 18 miles wide. The Grand Canyon is known for its stunning geological formations, colorful layers of rock, and breathtaking views. It was designated a national park in 1919 and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, attracting millions of visitors each year.

Visitors can explore the canyon by hiking, rafting, or taking scenic drives along the rims. The South Rim is the most accessible and offers numerous viewpoints and visitor facilities.

  • Location: Arizona, USA
  • Length: 277 miles
  • Width: Up to 18 miles
  • Established: National Park in 1919
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site
