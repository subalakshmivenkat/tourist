Great Barrier Reef

About the Great Barrier Reef

The Great Barrier Reef is the world's largest coral reef system and one of the most stunning natural wonders.

Located off the coast of Queensland, Australia, it stretches over 2,300 kilometers (1,430 miles) and comprises approximately 2,900 individual reefs and 900 islands. It is home to a diverse range of marine life, including thousands of species of fish, corals, and other sea creatures.

The reef is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and offers a variety of activities for visitors, including snorkeling, scuba diving, and boat tours, providing an opportunity to experience its breathtaking underwater ecosystems.

  • Location: Queensland, Australia
  • Size: Over 344,400 square kilometers (133,000 square miles)
  • Established: 1981 as a UNESCO World Heritage Site
  • Best Time to Visit: June to October for ideal weather
  • Activities: Snorkeling, diving, boat tours, and scenic flights

Opening Hours

The reef is open year-round, with various tours operating daily.

Visiting Tips

  • Book tours in advance, especially during peak season.
  • Wear reef-safe sunscreen to protect marine life.
  • Stay hydrated and bring snacks for longer excursions.
  • Respect the reef by following guidelines and not touching marine life.
