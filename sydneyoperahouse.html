Sydney Opera House

About the Sydney Opera House

The Sydney Opera House is one of the most recognizable and iconic structures in the world.

Located on Bennelong Point in Sydney Harbour, it was designed by Danish architect Jørn Utzon and opened in 1973. The building's unique shell-like design has made it a masterpiece of modern architecture and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

It serves as a multi-venue performing arts center, hosting a variety of events, including opera, ballet, theater, and concerts. The site also offers guided tours, providing visitors with insights into its architectural significance and history.

  • Architect: Jørn Utzon
  • Opened: 1973
  • Location: Bennelong Point, Sydney, Australia
  • Style: Modern architecture
  • Capacity: Over 5,000 seats in various venues

Opening Hours

Daily: 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM (hours may vary for performances).

Visiting Tips

  • Book tickets for performances in advance.
  • Join a guided tour for a deeper understanding of the site.
  • Explore the surrounding gardens and harbor views.
  • Visit the nearby Royal Botanic Garden for a scenic walk.
